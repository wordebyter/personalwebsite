Exploring some use cases of the Foundational ASSIST dataset.

Published: April 13, 2026 | Category: Datasets

This post will explore some potential use cases of the Foundational ASSIST dataset, and hopefully aid researchers in figuring out what they hope to investigate with the dataset, and how to do so.

Stability of misconceptions.

There is a long standing belief that misconceptions are stable. This means that given two similar problems, you should expect to see students misconceptions remain stable. Consider the following two problems:

What is the surface area of this rectangular prism? [alt text: 2 by 4 by 4 unit rectangular prism.]
A rectangular prism is 4 units high, 2 units wide, and 6 units long. What is its surface area in square units?

Suppose a student does not understand the difference between volume and surface area. You might then expect a student to answer "32" for the first problem, and "48" for the second.

The results suggest some stability. A lot of students gave both the "32" and "48" answer, but certainly not all. 206 students answered "32" for the first problem, and 98 continued to answer "48" on the second. Thus, I claim misconceptions are about 50% stable. Perhaps this is a weak claim, with very little evidence, but I hope this dataset and example might help others begin to analyze misconceptions. You might also look at whether these misconceptions only persist for a certain period of time, (using interactions['end_time']), across certain skills (using the skills dataset), or across more than 2 problems, using the 3353 unique problems, of which you could likely find many with (or without) persistent, stable, misconceptions.
