About Me

I am a PhD student at Worcester Polytechnic Institute advised by Prof. Neil Heffernan. My research lies at the intersection of artificial intelligence, data science, and education. I develop and evaluate LLM-based systems (prompt engineering, RAG, fine-tuning, RLHF) to provide scalable, high-quality support to students in online learning platforms such as ASSISTments.

Recent work includes running randomized controlled trials (RCTs) showing that AI-generated explanations match expert quality and improve student learning, curating novel educational datasets for LLM benchmarking, and fine-tuning models on challenging reasoning tasks (e.g., NP-completeness reductions).

Outside research, I enjoy homebrewing, running, board games, and reading fantasy (currently obsessed with The Empyrean series).

Research Highlights

  • Ran RCTs demonstrating AI-generated step-by-step explanations are as effective as expert-authored ones for middle-school math learning — and far better than direct answers.
  • Curated FoundationalASSIST, a 1.7M-interaction dataset with full question text, student responses, distractors, and Common Core alignment — enabling new LLM research on knowledge tracing and pedagogical reasoning (dataset forthcoming).
  • Created the Karp dataset of detailed NP-completeness reduction proofs; used it to fine-tune LLMs and improve mathematical reasoning capabilities.
